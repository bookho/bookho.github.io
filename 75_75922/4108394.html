    The train itself is undoubtedly much more antique than the modern railway. A complete front is divided into two sections. The first half consists mainly of the fourth-generation steam engine and transmission, and the second half is the coal storage vehicle. The cab is in the middle. In addition to controlling the speed and sounding the whistle, it is also the driver's responsibility to add coal to the boiler by manpower.

    Although the train is affected by the expedition, it is still in the semi-finished stage, but it is still much better than the other world's first steam locomotive. For example, steam directly works on the crankshaft rather than through the flywheel belt, and on both sides of the wheel, the linkage mechanism without dead ends is used instead of the single-wheel drive by the gear set.
