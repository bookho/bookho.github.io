    In accordance with the construction plan, Roland arranged a equipment room at each well point to accommodate all the equipment needed for the three links for management.

    The complete equipment consists of a steam engine, an electric motor, a power supply, a boiler and two storage tanks. Adding a water tower outside the room constitutes the entire three-way system.

    The steam engine is responsible for pumping well water into the water tower and the storage tank. The former mainly supplies domestic water to the house, and the latter is the heating water for the boiler. The first-class reservoir is higher than the ground, and there are limestone and washing stones suspended in the tank for precipitating magnesium and calcium ions in the water to play the role of soft water. The clarified water will be introduced into a lower-level secondary storage tank, and the floating material in the water will be stopped by a special filter made by Soroya.

    The water supply to the boiler is completed by the electric motor. When the internal water level is too low, the float valve will be driven to open the motor to deliver water until the water level returns to normal. This process is more artificial than manual Adding or steam engine delivery is much more stable, and it eliminates the need to watch the station at any time.
