     Another thing to be happy about is that the castle is about to come from the age of water.

    The towering water tower stands in the backyard of the castle. The tower is welded by an iron frame and is triangular. The top of the tower is connected with a steel two meters in diameter and three meters high. Bucket and bucket with float valve for extremely convenient water level control. The tower was welded by Anna, and then covered with a rust-proof coating by Soroya.     Because of the height of the castle, the water tower in the backyard is higher than the Chishui River, which is close to 12 meters, almost the same as the top of the castle.     The steam engine is located outside the backyard for water supply to the water tower, which comes from the well in the backyard of the castle. Calculated, this is already the third generation of steam engines, the biggest improvement is the great reduction of noise pollution.

    At each connection point of the steam engine, a gasket is added - this light blue gasket is what Sorosa draws when it is in the sky The color is very soft and tough, which makes the vibration noise of the machine work greatly reduced. The inside of the cylinder and the edge of the piston are covered with grass color, which reduces air leakage and improves work efficiency. Parts that are prone to chattering, such as exhaust ducts, are all replaced with coated hoses.

    In order to prevent the steam engine from being exposed to the sun and rain, and to further isolate the noise, Roland also built a small room around it. The walls inside the house were painted by Soroya. The upper layer of honeycomb is painted and used as a porous sound absorbing material. The actual test results show that even if you run at night, the noise of the machine will not wake up the sleeping people in the castle.
