    At the bottom of the chaotic combination of technology and magic, follow a very simple and striking world rule: and this criterion is exactly the opposite of the fantasy formula that starts with lotr. of. Since Tolkien’s decline, most of the epic fantasy works have a very obvious feature: in the world view, there is a “good era that has passed away” or “the lost ancient advanced civilization” – in a word It means that "the past is better than modern." The Lord of the Rings itself is a beautiful world that is fictionalized to fill the ancient myths, not to mention; Ista of the Dragon Gun, the real city of Amber, the colorless empire of Feilun, and the ancient civilizations of countless games and novels. "Ancient language magic", "Lost Atlantis"... All of this can be said to be the image of the Christian Eden, or the longing for the good times of the past.

    But my world is set differently: in this worldview, there is an ancient empire that perishes, but there is absolutely no "strong civilization." The basis of bis's worldview is "rationality."

    Yes, "rational." Bis's worldview recognizes the butterfly effect, does not recognize the existence of fate; recognizes the laws of history and does not recognize historical necessity. Bis's worldview allows magic to exist, allowing religion to exist, but it does not recognize creationism and recognizes natural selection. The world of bis has a god, but it is not God-made, but artificial. Everything is set to follow rationality. Here, the curse is not an ancient curse, but is gradually developed with the development of the times; spiritual power is not the enemy of science, but the two wings of scientific methods. The magic technology of the old times is always not as strong as the new era. This is a magical world that is constantly improving. It does not stay in a fixed era forever, but keeps moving forward.

    Besides the setting of a very idealistic philosophy: In this world, the spirit determines the power. The power of magic is determined only by the strength of the belief. The sword does not suppress the pen, and power and violence cannot suppress wisdom and thought. Because there is magic as the blade of the wise, because there is magic as a gun of knowledge.
